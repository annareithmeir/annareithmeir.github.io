Predicting secondary structure of Proteins with Neural Networks and Multitask learning

04/19    

Neural networks have proven to be useful for the prediction of the secondary structure of proteins from multiple sequence alignments. As the computation of such alignments is computationally complex, I looked at the possibilities to achieve similar secondary structure predictions by using methods from NLP and advanced network models. I represented the proteins by word embeddings and studied if the prediction can benefit from the additional learning of structural features, namely the relative solvent accessibility and the flexibility. To this end, I trained, tested and evaluated different network architectures on a data set of 2935 protein sequences. Apart from CNNs and LSTMs, I implemented combinations of the two network types as well as networks with DenseNet-inspired layers. I could find out that a Q3 accuracy of 83.17% can be achieved on that specific dataset and that learning of additional structural protein features is possible without a significant loss of performance.
